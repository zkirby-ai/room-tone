# Room Tone — Product Spec

## Summary
Room Tone is a mobile-first PWA that helps people get conversations going at parties, dinners, mixers, and late-night hangs.

It is not a charisma coach and not a networking tool. It is a **social momentum product**:
- break awkward openings
- rescue flat 1:1 chats
- give groups a prompt or tiny game structure
- keep the room lively without feeling forced or cringe

## Product goal
Help users create better social energy in real time with low-friction prompts, group mechanics, and pass-the-phone social scaffolding.

## Core problem
At gatherings, the failure mode usually is not “nobody knows how to speak.”
It is:
- boring small talk loops
- no easy way to widen a conversation
- dead air after the first exchange
- a room that needs shape or momentum

The app should solve that without making the user look like they opened an HR workshop app.

## Product principles
- Low cringe is the whole game
- Prompts should be easy to say out loud
- Tone matters more than technical complexity
- Curated beats AI slop for v1
- Social play beats self-improvement framing
- Mobile-first, one-hand usable, fast to open

## Intended user
Primary:
- socially active people at parties / dinners / hangs
- hosts who want to improve room energy
- anyone who wants a better way past bad small talk

Secondary:
- dates / 1:1 hangs
- friend groups
- hosts / facilitators

## Product shape
### Modes
1. **Spark**
   - single prompt generator
   - best for opening new conversations

2. **Group**
   - prompts for 3+ people
   - creates shared orbit quickly

3. **Rescue**
   - 1:1 dead-air recovery prompts
   - less boring than “what do you do?”

4. **Games**
   - low-rules, pass-the-phone or go-around interactions
   - good for resetting room energy

## Current MVP scope
- curated prompt deck
- mode tabs
- one-tap next prompt
- save favorites locally
- dark, social-night aesthetic
- installable PWA shell later in setup

## What makes it good
### The real edge
Taste.

If the prompts feel lame, too intimate, or too workshop-coded, the product dies.
If they feel witty, safe, and socially alive, the product works.

### Prompt quality rules
Prompts should be:
- easy to answer aloud
- a little surprising
- socially safe
- likely to create stories
- not too serious too fast
- not fake-deep

## Feature roadmap
### v1
- Spark / Group / Rescue / Games modes
- curated prompts
- saved prompts
- pass-the-phone friendliness

### v2
- vibe filters: chill / funny / flirty / chaotic / late-night
- host mode
- party size selectors
- random packs
- better saved prompt organization

### v3
- user-submitted prompt packs
- AI-assisted prompt expansion using strong curation guardrails
- host energy tools like “loop in quieter people” or “reset the room”

## Design direction
- dark, slightly neon nightlife energy
- stylish but not clubby
- strong contrast, high readability in low light
- playful without becoming juvenile

## Non-goals
- corporate networking coaching
- therapy-style social support
- generic self-help
- overengineered chat AI assistant mode

## Success criteria
The app wins if:
- prompts actually get used out loud
- users save specific bangers
- people feel the app improves room energy
- it becomes something people open during real gatherings, not just test once alone
